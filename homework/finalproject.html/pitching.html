•What exactly is "pitching" a script?•

Pitching a script is when a screenwriter briefly presents an idea for a film to a producer or studio executive, hoping for it to be financed.

•How many pitches are there generally?•

There are generally two forms of pitch. One is the elevator pitch and the second is the story pitch.

•What is the elevator pitch?•

The elevator pitch takes normally 60 seconds to deliver. Anything above ninety seconds is too detailed. Be sure to always have it prepared, just in case.

•What is the story pitch?•

The story pitch discusses your characters, their goals, conflict, turning points, and the conclusion. These are often seen in general meetings.

•Which producer should you pitch your idea to?•

Always make sure to check what the producer has produced in the past and who their key players are.
